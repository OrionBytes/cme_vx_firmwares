CME Announces UF Design Contest Beijing, China (June 6, 2006) - CME, based in China and distributed in the US by Yamaha Corporation of America, announces a new graphic design contest for their popular UF Series Master Keyboard Controllers. The UF Series’ striking red all-metal chassis first caught the music industry’s eye at the 2005 Winter NAMM show. Today, customers can use CME’s downloadable design template to create their own custom graphics for the keyboard and enter to win a CME VX7, the newest edition in the company’s line of powerful controller/interfaces. One Grand Prize winner will receive a CME VX7, the world’s first keyboard controller with 9 motorized 60mm faders. The VX7 also features a built in USB audio interface and 64 different types of programmable controllers to control a DAW and virtual instruments in the studio, trigger patterns and control sound rigs on stage, and record audio into a laptop while on the go. One Second Prize winner will receive a CME BitStream 3X. This compact desktop unit is the only 3 axis MIDI controller on the market and includes 35 knobs, 8 sliders, 16 buttons, a joystick, a cross-fader and an infrared controller. The Bitstream 3X is perfect for controlling Virtual Instrument, DAWs, and even other MIDI hardware. One Third Prize winner will receive a CME U-key mobile-tone keyboard and four qualifying entries will receive one of the following: CME Matrix X, CME Matrix Y, or CME Xcorpio. For entry information and examples, please visit www.cme-pro.com.
